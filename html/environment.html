Project Destruction is a game based in and around a city environment.

There are roads, large buildings, intersections and plazas, along with other features that make the environment look and feel more like an actual city.

The game's environment is basically split into two categories: Buildings(indoor) and Organic/Inorganic(outdoors - trees, light posts, bushes etc).

The theme of Project Destruction is Procedural generation.

Project Destruction's buildings are procedurally generated and the props inside the buildings are procedurally generated as well.

This means that each time you play the game, the buildings, props, weapon locations and overall experience will be different each time you play!



Buildings (and roads):


Each building is essentially like a skyscraper that has a random height and random base size.

Each of the walls can be destroyed, and the building itself can collapse if it takes enough damage from outside events.

Each building has a differing length width and height making its size unique, as seen in the photo above. Roads also seperate each of the city blocks

There are three types of rooms in each building: Office, Apartment and Restaurant.

The Restaurants are generally towards the bottom of the building while the Apartments and Offices are towards the top.

This means that all of the office props and apartment props will be seen higher up in the buildings versus down lower.



Organic and Inorganic:

The Organic environment includes typical things found in nature, such as trees bushes brass etc.

These organic objects will be found in the plazas that are placed in between buildings.


The Inogranic environment includes things found in cities, such as light posts, large billboards, etc.

These inorganic objects will be found around the city blocks and buildings alike.
